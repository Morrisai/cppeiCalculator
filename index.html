When will I max out my CPP and EI contributions?

CPP

With very few exceptions, every person over the age of 18, who work in Canada outside of Quebec and earns more than a minimum amount ($3,500 per year) must contribute to the Canada Pension Plan (CPP). [1]

You make contributions only on your annual earnings between a minimum and a maximum amount (these are called your pensionable earnings). [2]

For 2016, the maximum amount is $54,900.

EI

Employment Insurance (EI) provides temporary financial assistance to unemployed Canadians who have lost their job through no fault of their own, while they look for work or upgrade their skills. [3]

The maximum insurable earnings for 2016 is $50,800.

In a basic sense, once you have reached the maximum contribution for the year you will see an increase on your net pay going forward as these deductions will no longer be applied.
